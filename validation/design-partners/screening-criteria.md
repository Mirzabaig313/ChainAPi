# Design Partner Screening Criteria

> Filter the candidate list before reaching out. Each candidate must hit **at least 3 of the 4** core criteria below.

## Core criteria

| # | Criterion | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Backend or full-stack engineer** with 2+ years professional experience | The MVP is built around their workflow. Frontend-only devs are not the audience. |
| 2 | **Currently uses an HTTP client** (Postman, Bruno, Insomnia, ThunderClient, curl + scripts) regularly — daily or near-daily | If they don't have the pain, they can't validate the cure. |
| 3 | **Works on a multi-actor / multi-role API** (admin + user, vendor + customer, multi-tenant SaaS, B2B platform) | This is the core wedge. Single-actor APIs don't surface the pain ChainAPI solves. |
| 4 | **Has 50+ requests in their current Postman/Bruno/Insomnia workspace** | Validates that their workflow is mature enough that organization is a real problem. |

A candidate is **qualified** if they meet ≥ 3 of these. Mark borderline candidates and prefer the clearer fits when scheduling.

## Diversity targets

Across the 10 interviews, aim for at least:
- **4 from companies with 10–100 engineers** (the primary persona)
- **2 from companies <10 engineers** (small-team / solo persona)
- **2 QA-focused engineers** (the secondary persona — they validate the value prop from a different angle)
- **2 from any other relevant background** (DevRel, API platform team, internal tools team)

Having 10 interviews from the same company size and role gives a falsely homogeneous answer.

## Disqualifiers

Skip candidates who:

- Work primarily in a frontend / mobile UI role (they don't author API tests)
- Don't write or test their own backend code (e.g. pure architects or non-coding managers)
- Have a financial or competitive conflict (work at Postman, Bruno, Insomnia, Apidog, etc.)
- Are likely to give a positive answer out of politeness rather than conviction (close personal friends — bias risk too high; use as practice runs but don't count toward the 10)

## Scoring during screening

Track in a simple spreadsheet:

| Name | Company | Role | Headcount | Has API tool pain? (yes/no) | Multi-role API? (yes/no) | Qualified? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Pre-fill from public info (LinkedIn, GitHub) before sending outreach. The fewer questions you ask up front, the higher the response rate.
